Cringe, cliche-ridden, sponcon-heavy, but ultimately endearing and maybe a post-modern trojan horse. Peel back the vapid soundtrack and on-the-nose intergenerational dialogue about "not looking at your phone" to reveal a pretty sincere message about the shallowness of celebrity and meaning to be found by focusing on the "doing". Kind of a taoist text. The characters feel like avatars in service to something more essential. In my book, nailing that mystic, transcendental quality of a sport is so much more important than the surface-level realism for anoraks. Not bad.

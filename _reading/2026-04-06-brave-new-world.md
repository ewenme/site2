Stirred up some spooky feels like *Terminal Boredom* did. Almost one hundred years old and confusingly fresh. The emptiness of pursuing a frictionless, convenience-led existence read like a parable purpose built for resisting the dull and sexless worldview of big tech and hollywood today. A deep-seated fear of introspection and subversiveness that feeds an addiction to self-soothing is eerie. Similarly, the repulsion to ageing reminded me of obsessive wellness and invasive medical management being normalised in the 21st century. Good shit and I need to go finish the *Tibetan Book of the Dead*.

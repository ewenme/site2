Probably the best autobiography I've read? All of the commitment to truth and messiness that was missing from Rodigan's. I saw the Spike Lee film adaptation before reading the source material, so I was doing the Leo-points-at-TV meme quite a lot when a scene sprung up. There really is a poetic arc that follows the formation of his ideas and unwavering integrity. He leaves everything on these pages. I think people who have had brushes with death tend to operate on a different frequency and you feel it.

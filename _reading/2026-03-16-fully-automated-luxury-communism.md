Reading a bold, techno-optimist manifesto six or seven years after release is not a bad way to judge the strength of the arguments within. There are a lot of claims in here about technological shifts to come in the twenties and beyond. A decent chunk (including AI advancements) are on track, which is noteworthy. Some, like asteroid mining, seem a bit behind schedule.

I think the book does a good job of presenting exciting technological progress and the emancipatory potential within, but is pretty light on details when it comes to how the liberation will follow in practice. A bit of hand-waving about universal basic services (UBS) etc. It's just nice to read progressive literature that goes past pointing out the shitheads.
